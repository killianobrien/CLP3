Step 1: List explicitly all the functions involved and specify the arguments of each function. Ensure that all different functions have different names. Invent new names for some of the functions if necessary. In the case of the chain rule in Theorem 2.4.1, the list would be
\begin{equation*} f(x,y)\qquad\qquad x(s,t)\qquad\qquad y(s,t)\qquad\qquad F(s,t)=f\big(x(s,t),y(s,t)\big) \end{equation*}
While the functions \(f\) and \(F\) are closely related, they are not the same. One is a function of \(x\) and \(y\) while the other is a function of \(s\) and \(t\text{.}\)
