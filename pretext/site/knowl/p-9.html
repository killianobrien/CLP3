The method described here, Laplace transforms, can often find the exact solution to a linear ordinary differential equation with constant coefficients, i.e. one of the form

\begin{equation*} a_n \frac{d^ny}{dt^n} + \dots + a_1 \frac{dy}{dt} + a_0y = g(t), \end{equation*}

where \(y\) is the unknown function whose solution is sought. Note that we are allowed a non-constant function \(g\) on the right hand side.
