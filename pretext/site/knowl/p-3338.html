(a) On the domain of integration

  • \(y\) runs from \(0\) to \(\sqrt{2}\) and
  • for each \(y\) in that range, \(x\) runs from \(y\) to \(\sqrt{4-y^2}\text{.}\) We can rewrite \(x=\sqrt{4-y^2}\) in the more familiar form \(x^2+y^2=4\text{,}\) \(x\ge 0\text{.}\)
