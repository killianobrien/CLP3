The orthodox procedure for evaluating the resulting trigonometric integral \(\int_0^{\pi/2} \cos^2\theta\ \dee{\theta}\text{,}\) covered in ยง1.8 of the CLP-2 text, uses the trigonometric double angle formula

\begin{equation*} \cos(2\theta) = 2\cos^2\theta -1\qquad\text{to write}\qquad \cos^2\theta =\frac{1+\cos(2\theta)}{2} \end{equation*}

and then

\begin{align*} \int_0^a \sqrt{a^2-x^2}\ \dee{x} &=a^2\int_0^{\pi/2} \cos^2\theta\ \dee{\theta} =\frac{a^2}{2} \int_0^{\pi/2} \big[1+\cos(2\theta)\big]\ \dee{\theta}\\ &=\frac{a^2}{2}\left[\theta+\frac{\sin(2\theta)}{2}\right]_0^{\pi/2}\\ &=\frac{\pi a^2}{4} \end{align*}
