Solution 1.4.1.2.1.
  1. Each point on the \(y\)-axis is of the form \((0,y,0)\text{.}\) Such a point is on the plane \(P\) if
    \begin{equation*} 3(0)+\frac{1}{2}y+0=4 \iff y=8 \end{equation*}
    So the intersection of \(P\) with the \(y\)-axis is the single point \((0,8,0)\text{.}\)
  2. Each point on the \(z\)-axis is of the form \((0,0,z)\text{.}\) Such a point is on the plane \(P\) if
    \begin{equation*} 3(0)+\frac{1}{2}(0)+z=4 \iff z=4 \end{equation*}
    So the intersection of \(P\) with the \(z\)-axis is the single point \((0,0,4)\text{.}\)

  3. The intersection of the plane \(P\) with the \(yz\)-plane is a line. We have shown in parts (a) and (b) that the points \((0,8,0)\) and \((0,0,4)\) are on that line. Here is a sketch of the part of that line that is in the first octant.
