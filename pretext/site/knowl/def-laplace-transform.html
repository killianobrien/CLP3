Definition 1.2.1. Laplace transform.

Let \(f\) be a function defined on the positive real numbers. The Laplace transform of a function \(f\) is a function of a new variable \(s\) and is defined by the integral

\begin{equation*} \trans{f(t)} . \end{equation*}

Various notations are commonly used for the Laplace transform of \(f\text{.}\) It can be denoted by any of

\begin{equation*} \L{f(t)}, \, \, \bar{f}(s) \text{ or } F(s). \end{equation*}
