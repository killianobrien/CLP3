Perhaps the best known function whose antiderivative cannot be expressed in terms of elementary functions is \(e^{-x^2} \text{.}\) It is the integrand of the error function \(\erf(x) =\frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}}\int_0^x e^{-t^2}\ dt\) that is used in computing “bell curve” probabilities.
