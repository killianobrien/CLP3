Item 2.4.5.23.a.
Let \(f\) be an arbitrary differentiable function defined on the entire real line. Show that the function \(w\) defined on the entire plane as
\begin{equation*} w(x,y)=e^{-y}f(x-y) \end{equation*}
satisfies the partial differential equation:
\begin{equation*} w+\pdiff{w}{x}+\pdiff{w}{y}=0 \end{equation*}
