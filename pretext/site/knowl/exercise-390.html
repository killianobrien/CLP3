Exercise 7.

Use polar coordinates to evaluate each of the following integrals.

  1. \(\displaystyle\dblInt_S (x+y)\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) where \(S\) is the region in the first quadrant lying inside the disc \(x^2+y^2\le a^2\) and under the line \(y=\sqrt{3}x\text{.}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle\dblInt_Sx\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y}\text{,}\) where \(S\) is the disc segment \(x^2+y^2\le 2,\ x\ge 1\text{.}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle\dblInt_T (x^2+y^2)\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) where \(T\) is the triangle with vertices \((0,0), (1,0)\) and \((1,1)\text{.}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1} \ln(x^2+y^2)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\)
