Exercise Group 2.8.3.11–14.

Exercises — Stage 3

11.

The distance from the point \((x,y,z)\) to the origin \((0,0,0)\) is

\begin{equation*} r(x,y,z) = \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2} \end{equation*}

Find all functions \(u(x,y,z) = r(x,y,z)^n \text{,}\) with \(n\) being a real constant, that obey Laplace's equation

\begin{equation*} u_{xx}+u_{yy}+u_{zz}=0 \end{equation*}

for all \((x,y,z)\ne (0,0,0)\text{.}\)

12.

In this question we are going to find all solutions \(u(t,x)\) to the PDE

\begin{equation*} u_t=xu_x\qquad\text{for }x>0 \end{equation*}

that are of the special form \(u(x,t)= X(x)\,T(t)\text{,}\) with, for simplicity, \(X>0\) and \(T>0\text{.}\) We will use a technique called “separation of variables”.

  1. Show that \(u(x,t)= X(x)\,T(t)\text{,}\) with \(X\) and \(T\) nonzero, obeys the PDE \(u_t=xu_x\) if and only if
    \begin{equation*} \frac{T'(t)}{T(t)} = x\frac{X'(x)}{X(x)} \end{equation*}
  2. Show that \(\frac{T'(t)}{T(t)} = x\frac{X'(x)}{X(x)}\) if and only if there is a constant \(\la\) such that
    \begin{align*} T'(t)\amp=\la T(t) \\ X'(x)\amp=\frac{\la}{x} X(x) \end{align*}
  3. Find the general solutions to \(T'(t)=\la T(t)\) and \(X'(x)=\frac{\la}{x} X(x)\) with \(T,X>0\text{.}\)
13.

Suppose that \(u(x,y)\) obeys the PDE

\begin{equation*} \al(x,y)\,u_x(x,y) +\be(x,y)\,u_y(x,y)=0 \end{equation*}

where \(\al(x,y)\) and \(\be(x,y)\) are given functions. Let \(\big(X(t),Y(t)\big)\) be a curve 7  in the \(xy\)-plane that obeys

\begin{align*} \diff{X}{t}(t)\amp=\al\big(X(t),Y(t)\big) \\ \diff{Y}{t}(t)\amp=\be\big(X(t),Y(t)\big) \end{align*}

Show that \(u\) is constant along that curve. That is, show that \(u\big(X(t),Y(t)\big)\) is independent of \(t\text{.}\)

14.
  1. Suppose that \(u(x,y)\) obeys the PDE
    \begin{equation*} 3u_x(x,y) + 6u_y(x,y)=u(x,y)\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Define \(v(X,Y) = u(X, Y+2X)\text{.}\) Find a PDE that \(v\) obeys.
  2. Suppose that \(u(x,y)\) obeys the PDE
    \begin{equation*} xu_x(x,y) + yu_y(x,y)=u(x,y)\text{.} \end{equation*}
    Define \(v(X,Y) = u(X, Xe^Y)\text{.}\) Find a PDE that \(v\) obeys.
