We are told that the four variables \(T\text{,}\) \(U\text{,}\) \(V\text{,}\) \(W\) obey the the single equation \((TU-V)^2 \ln(W-UV) = \ln 2\text{.}\) So they are not all independent variables. Roughly speaking, we can treat any three of them as independent variables and solve the given equation for the fourth as a function of the three chosen independent variables.
