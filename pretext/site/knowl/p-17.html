The Fourier coefficients for a given function \(f\) can be found by evaluating the integral formulae.

\begin{align*} a_0 \amp = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^\pi f(x) \, dx,\\ a_n \amp = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^\pi f(x) \cos(nx) \, dx, \quad (n \geq 1)\\ b_n \amp = \frac{1}{\pi} \int_{-\pi}^\pi f(x) \sin(nx) \, dx \quad (n \geq 1). \end{align*}

These can be justified by starting with the Fourier series for \(f\) and applying the orthogonality property of the inner product.
