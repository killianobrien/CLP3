Under the hypotheses of Theorem 3.1.3,

  1. If \(f(x,y)\ge 0\) for all \((x,y)\) in \(\cR\text{,}\) then
    \begin{gather*} \dblInt_\cR f(x,y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \ge 0 \end{gather*}
  2. If there are constants \(m\) and \(M\) such that \(m\le f(x,y)\le M\) for all \((x,y)\) in \(\cR\text{,}\) then
    \begin{gather*} m\,\text{Area}(\cR)\le \dblInt_\cR f(x,y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \le M\,\text{Area}(\cR) \end{gather*}
  3. If \(f(x,y)\le g(x,y)\) for all \((x,y)\) in \(\cR\text{,}\) then
    \begin{gather*} \dblInt_\cR f(x,y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \le \dblInt_\cR g(x,y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{gather*}
  4. We have
    \begin{gather*} \left|\dblInt_\cR f(x,y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\right| \le \dblInt_\cR |f(x,y)|\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{gather*}
