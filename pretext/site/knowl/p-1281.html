If \(f_y(0,0) \lt 0\text{,}\) then \(f(0,y)\) decreases as \(y\) increases from \(0\text{.}\) Thus moving in the positive \(y\) direction takes you downhill. This means you aren't at the lowest point in a valley, since you can still move downhill. On the other hand, as \(f_y(0,0) \lt 0\text{,}\) \(f(0,y)\) also decreases as \(y\) increases towards \(0\) from slightly negative values.
