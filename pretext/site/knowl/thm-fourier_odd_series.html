Theorem 5.4.6. Fourier series of odd functions.

Suppose that \(h\) is an odd function with a Fourier series. Then its series takes the form

\begin{equation*} h(x) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty b_n \sin(nx), \end{equation*}

i.e. the cosine and constant Fourier coefficients are all zero. Moreover the \(b_n\) can be given by the formula

\begin{equation*} b_n = \frac{2}{\pi} \int_0^\pi g(x) \sin(nx) . \end{equation*}
