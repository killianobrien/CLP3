In the solution of the ODE

\begin{equation*} \frac{d^2 y}{dt^2} + 4y = 10 e^{-t} + 8, \end{equation*}

subject to \(y(0)=7\) and \(y'(0)=-2\) we get

\begin{equation*} y(t) = 3 \cos(2t) + 2 e^{-t} + 2. \end{equation*}

As \(t \to \infty\) the first and last term of \(y\) will continue to have an effect on \(y\text{,}\) however the middle exponential term decays (converges) to \(0\text{.}\) We call such terms in a solution that converge to 0 as \(t \to \infty\text{,}\) transient terms. The other terms, which do not decay, are called steady state terms.
