add equations (E1) and (E2) together. This gives
\begin{equation*} (E1) + (E2):\ \ (1-1)x+(-1+2)y = -1+3 \iff y=2 \end{equation*}
The point here is that adding equations (E1) and (E2) together eliminates the unknown \(x\text{,}\) leaving us with one equation in the unknown \(y\text{,}\) which is easily solved. For other systems of equations you might have to multiply the equations by some numbers before adding them together.
