Footnote 3.1.3.
Think of the part of \(\cV\) that is above the strip as being a thin slice of bread. Then the factor \(\dee{y}\) in \(\dee{y}\int_{L(y)}^{R(y)}\dee{x}\ f(x,y)\) is the thickness of the slice of bread. The factor \(\int_{L(y)}^{R(y)}\dee{x}\ f(x,y)\) is the surface area of the constant \(y\) cross-section \(\Set{(x,z)}{L(y)\le x\le R(y),\ 0\le z\le f(x,y)}\text{,}\) i.e. the surface area of the slice of bread.
