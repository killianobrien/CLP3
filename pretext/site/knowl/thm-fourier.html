Theorem 1.1.1.

If \(f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}\) is a periodic function of period \(2 \pi\) and in the interval \((-\pi, \pi)\) has

  1. at most a finite number of discontinuities ,

  2. at most a finite number of maxima and minima,

then there exist a set of coefficients

\begin{equation*} a_0, a_1, a_2, \dots , b_1, b_2 , \dots \in \mathbb{R}, \end{equation*}

such that at any point of \(x\) of continuity of \(f\) we have

\begin{equation*} f(x) = \frac{1}{2} a_0 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n \cos(nx) + \sum_{n=1}^\infty b_n \sin(nx), \end{equation*}

and at points of discontinuity, \(x_d\text{,}\) the series satisfies

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{2} \left ( f(x_d^+) + f(x_d^-) \right ) = \frac{1}{2} a_0 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n \cos(nx) + \sum_{n=1}^\infty b_n \sin(nx), \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} f(x_d^+) = \lim_{x \downarrow x_d} f(x), \quad f(x_d^-) = \lim_{x \uparrow x_d} f(x). \end{equation*}
