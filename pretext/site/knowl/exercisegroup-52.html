Exercise Group 3.1.7.3–28.

Exercises — Stage 2

Questions 3.1.7.3 through 3.1.7.8 provide practice with limits of integration for double integrals in Cartesian coordinates.

3.

For each of the following, evaluate the given double integral using iteration.

  1. \(\displaystyle\dblInt_R (x^2+y^2)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) where \(R\) is the rectangle \(0\le x\le a,\ 0\le y\le b\) where \(a \gt 0\) and \(b \gt 0\text{.}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle\dblInt_T (x-3y)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) where \(T\) is the triangle with vertices \((0,0),\ (a,0),\ (0,b)\text{.}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle\dblInt_R xy^2\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) where \(R\) is the finite region in the first quadrant bounded by the curves \(y=x^2\) and \(x=y^2\text{.}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle\dblInt_D x\cos y\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) where \(D\) is the finite region in the first quadrant bounded by the coordinate axes and the curve \(y=1-x^2\text{.}\)
  5. \(\displaystyle\dblInt_R {x\over y}e^y\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) where \(R\) is the region \(0\le x\le 1,\ x^2\le y\le x\text{.}\)
  6. \(\displaystyle\dblInt_T {xy\over 1+x^4}\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) where \(T\) is the triangle with vertices \((0,0),\ (0,1),\ (1,1)\text{.}\)
4.

For each of the following integrals (i) sketch the region of integration, (ii) write an equivalent double integral with the order of integration reversed and (iii) evaluate both double integrals.

  1. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle\int_0^2\dee{x}\int_1^{e^x}\dee{y}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle\int_0^{\sqrt{2}}\dee{y} \int_{-\sqrt{4-2y^2}}^{\sqrt{4-2y^2}}\dee{x}\ y\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \displaystyle\int_{-2}^1 \dee{x}\int_{x^2+4x}^{3x+2}\dee{y}\)
5. ✳.

Combine the sum of the two iterated double integrals

\begin{equation*} \int_{y=0}^{y=1}\int_{x=0}^{x=y} f(x,y)\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} +\int_{y=1}^{y=2}\int_{x=0}^{x=2-y} f(x,y)\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{equation*}

into a single iterated double integral with the order of integration reversed.

6. ✳.

Consider the integral

\begin{equation*} \int_0^1\int_x^1 e^{x/y}\ \dee{y}\ \dee{x} \end{equation*}
  1. Sketch the domain of integration.
  2. Evaluate the integral by reversing the order of integration.
7. ✳.

The integral \(I\) is defined as

\begin{equation*} I =\dblInt_R f(x,y)\ \dee{A} = \int_1^{\sqrt{2}} \int_{1/y}^{\sqrt{y}} f(x,y)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y} +\int_{\sqrt{2}}^4 \int_{y/2}^{\sqrt{y}} f(x,y)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{equation*}
  1. Sketch the region \(R\text{.}\)
  2. Re--write the integral \(I\) by reversing the order of integration.
  3. Compute the integral \(I\) when \(f(x,y)= x/y\text{.}\)
8. ✳.

A region \(E\) in the \(xy\)--plane has the property that for all continuous functions f

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_E f(x,y)\,\dee{A} = \int_{x=-1}^{x=3}\left[\int_{y=x^2}^{y=2x+3} f(x,y) \dee{y}\right] \dee{x} \end{equation*}
  1. Compute \(\dblInt_E x\,\dee{A}\text{.}\)
  2. Sketch the region E.
  3. Set up \(\dblInt_E x\,\dee{A}\) as an integral or sum of integrals in the opposite order.
9. ✳.

Calculate the integral:

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_D \sin(y^2)\ \dee{A} \end{equation*}

where \(D\) is the region bounded by \(x + y = 0\text{,}\) \(2x - y = 0\text{,}\) and \(y = 4\text{.}\)

10. ✳.

Consider the integral

\begin{equation*} I = \int_0^1 \int_{\sqrt{y}}^1 \frac{\sin(\pi x^2)}{x}\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{equation*}
  1. Sketch the region of integration.
  2. Evaluate I.
11. ✳.

Let \(I\) be the double integral of the function \(f(x,y) = y^2 \sin xy\) over the triangle with vertices \((0, 0)\text{,}\) \((0, 1)\) and \((1, 1)\) in the \(xy\)--plane.

  1. Write \(I\) as an iterated integral in two different ways.
  2. Evaluate \(I\text{.}\)
12. ✳.

Find the volume \((V)\) of the solid bounded above by the surface

\begin{equation*} z = f (x,y) = e^{-x^2}, \end{equation*}

below by the plane \(z = 0\) and over the triangle in the \(xy\)--plane formed by the lines \(x = 1\text{,}\) \(y = 0\) and \(y = x\text{.}\)

13. ✳.

Consider the integral \(\displaystyle I=\int_0^1 \int_y^{2-y}\frac{y}{x} \ \dee{x}\,\dee{y}\text{.}\)

  1. Sketch the region of integration.
  2. Interchange the order of integration.
  3. Evaluate \(I\text{.}\)
14. ✳.

For the integral

\begin{equation*} I = \int_0^1 \int_{\sqrt{x}}^1 \sqrt{1+y^3}\ \dee{y}\,\dee{x} \end{equation*}
  1. Sketch the region of integration.
  2. Evaluate \(I\text{.}\)
15. ✳.
  1. \(D\) is the region bounded by the parabola \(y^2 = x\) and the line \(y = x - 2\text{.}\) Sketch \(D\) and evaluate \(J\) where
    \begin{equation*} J = \dblInt_D 3y\ \dee{A} \end{equation*}
  2. Sketch the region of integration and then evaluate the integral \(I\) :
    \begin{equation*} I = \int_0^4 \int_{\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{x}}^1 e^{y^3}\ \dee{y}\,\dee{x} \end{equation*}
16. ✳.

Consider the iterated integral

\begin{equation*} \int_{-4}^0\int_{\sqrt{-y}}^2 \cos(x^3)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{equation*}
  1. Draw the region of integration.
  2. Evaluate the integral.
17. ✳.
  1. Combine the sum of the iterated integrals
    \begin{equation*} I = \int_0^1\int_{-\sqrt{y}}^{\sqrt{y}} f(x,y)\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} + \int_1^4\int_{y-2}^{\sqrt{y}} f(x,y)\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{equation*}
    into a single iterated integral with the order of integration reversed.
  2. Evaluate \(I\) if \(f(x,y)=\frac{e^x}{2-x}\text{.}\)
18. ✳.

Let

\begin{equation*} I=\int_0^4\int_{\sqrt{y}}^{\sqrt{8-y}} f(x,y)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{equation*}
  1. Sketch the domain of integration.
  2. Reverse the order of integration.
  3. Evaluate the integral for \(f(x,y)=\frac{1}{(1+y)^2}\text{.}\)
19. ✳.

Evaluate

\begin{equation*} \int_{-1}^0 \int_{-2}^{2x} e^{y^2}\ \dee{y}\,\dee{x} \end{equation*}
20. ✳.

Let

\begin{equation*} I = \int_0^2 \int_0^x f(x,y)\ \dee{y}\,\dee{x} + \int_2^6 \int_0^{\sqrt{6-x}} f(x,y)\ \dee{y}\,\dee{x} \end{equation*}

Express \(I\) as an integral where we integrate first with respect to \(x\text{.}\)

21. ✳.

Consider the domain \(D\) above the \(x\)--axis and below parabola \(y = 1-x^2\) in the \(xy\)--plane.

  1. Sketch \(D\text{.}\)
  2. Express
    \begin{equation*} \dblInt_D f(x,y)\ \dee{A} \end{equation*}
    as an iterated integral corresponding to the order \(\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\text{.}\) Then express this integral as an iterated integral corresponding to the order \(\dee{y}\,\dee{x}\text{.}\)
  3. Compute the integral in the case \(f(x,y) = e^{x-(x^3/3)}\text{.}\)
22. ✳.

Let \(I=\int_0^1 \int_{x^2}^1 x^3\ \sin(y^3)\ \dee{y}\ \dee{x}\text{.}\)

  1. Sketch the region of integration in the \(xy\)--plane. Label your sketch sufficiently well that one could use it to determine the limits of double integration.
  2. Evaluate \(I\text{.}\)
23. ✳.

Consider the solid under the surface \(z=6-xy\text{,}\) bounded by the five planes \(x=0\text{,}\) \(x=3\text{,}\) \(y=0\text{,}\) \(y=3\text{,}\) \(z=0\text{.}\) Note that no part of the solid lies below the \(x\)--\(y\) plane.

  1. Sketch the base of the solid in the \(xy\)--plane. Note that it is not a square!
  2. Compute the volume of the solid.
24. ✳.

Evaluate the following integral:

\begin{equation*} \int_{-2}^2\int_{x^2}^4\cos\big(y^{3/2}\big)\ \dee{y}\,\dee{x} \end{equation*}
25. ✳.

Consider the volume above the \(xy\)-plane that is inside the circular cylinder \(x^2+y^2=2y\) and underneath the surface \(z=8+2xy\text{.}\)

  1. Express this volume as a double integral \(I\text{,}\) stating clearly the domain over which I is to be taken.
  2. Express in Cartesian coordinates, the double integral \(I\) as an iterated intergal in two different ways, indicating clearly the limits of integration in each case.
  3. How much is this volume?
26. ✳.

Evaluate the following integral:

\begin{equation*} \int_0^9\int_{\sqrt{y}}^3\sin(\pi x^3)\ \dee{x}\dee{y} \end{equation*}
27. ✳.

The iterated integral

\begin{equation*} I=\int_0^1\bigg[\int_{-\sqrt{x}}^{\sqrt{x}} \sin\big(y^3-3y\big)\,\dee{y}\bigg] \ \dee{x} \end{equation*}

is equal to \(\dblInt_R\sin\big(y^3-3y)\ dA\) for a suitable region \(R\) in the \(xy\)-plane.

  1. Sketch the region \(R\text{.}\)
  2. Write the integral \(I\) with the orders of integration reversed, and with suitable limits of integration.
  3. Find \(I\text{.}\)
28. ✳.

Find the double integral of the function \(f(x, y) = xy\) over the region bounded by \(y = x - 1\) and \(y^2 = 2x + 6\text{.}\)
