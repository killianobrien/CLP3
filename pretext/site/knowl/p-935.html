The intersection of our surface with that horizontal plane is a horizontal cross-section. Any point \((x,y,z)\) lying on that horizontal cross-section satisfies both

\begin{align*} &z=z_0\ \ \text{and}\ \ 4x^2+y^2-z^2=1\\ \iff &z=z_0\ \ \text{and}\ \ 4x^2+y^2=1+z_0^2 \end{align*}

Think of \(z_0\) as a constant. Then \(4x^2+y^2=1+z_0^2\) is a curve in the \(xy\)-plane. As \(1+z_0^2\) is a constant, the curve is an ellipse. To determine its semi-axes 2 , we observe that when \(y=0\text{,}\) we have \(x=\pm\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{1+z_0^2}\) and when \(x=0\text{,}\) we have \(y=\pm\sqrt{1+z_0^2}\text{.}\) So the curve is just an ellipse with \(x\) semi-axis \(\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{1+z_0^2}\) and \(y\) semi-axis \(\sqrt{1+z_0^2}\text{.}\) It's easy to sketch.
