Consider the three vectors

\begin{equation*} \va=\llt 1,1,0 \rgt\qquad \vb=\llt 1,0,1 \rgt \qquad \vc=\llt -1,1,1\rgt \end{equation*}

Their dot products

\begin{alignat*}{3} \va\cdot\vb & = \llt 1,1,0 \rgt \cdot \llt 1,0,1 \rgt && = 1\times 1 +1\times 0+0\times 1 && = 1\\ \va\cdot\vc & = \llt 1,1,0 \rgt \cdot \llt -1,1,1 \rgt && = 1\times(-1) +1\times 1+0\times 1 && = 0\\ \vb\cdot\vc & = \llt 1,0,1 \rgt \cdot \llt -1,1,1 \rgt && = 1\times(-1) +0\times 1+1\times 1 && = 0 \end{alignat*}

tell us that \(\vc\) is perpendicular to both \(\va\) and \(\vb\text{.}\) Since both \(|\va|=|\vb|=\sqrt{1^2+1^2+0^2}=\sqrt{2}\) the first dot product tells us that the angle, \(\theta\text{,}\) between \(\va\) and \(\vb\) obeys

\begin{gather*} \cos\theta =\frac{\va\cdot\vb}{|\va|\,|\vb|}=\frac{1}{2} \implies \theta =\frac{\pi}{3} \end{gather*}
