Suppose that functions \(P\) and \(Q\) have continuous partial derivatives on the closed contour \(C\) and in the simply connected region \(R\) enclosed by \(C\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \oint\limits_{C} \, P(x,y) \, dx + Q(x,y) \, dy = \iint\limits_{R} \, \left ( \pderiv{Q}{x} - \pderiv{P}{y} \right ) \, dx \, dy . \end{equation*}
