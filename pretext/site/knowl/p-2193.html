We simply evaluate the two terms on the left hand side when \(z=z(x,y)=\frac{x+y}{x-y}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} x\pdiff{z}{x}\amp= x\pdiff{}{x}\left(\frac{x+y}{x-y}\right) =x\frac{(1)(x-y)-(x+y)(1)}{(x-y)^2} =\frac{-2xy}{(x-y)^2} \\ y\pdiff{z}{y}\amp= y\pdiff{}{y}\left(\frac{x+y}{x-y}\right) =y\frac{(1)(x-y)-(x+y)(-1)}{(x-y)^2} =\frac{2xy}{(x-y)^2} \end{align*}

So

\begin{equation*} x\pdiff{z}{x}+y\pdiff{z}{y} =\frac{-2xy}{(x-y)^2} +\frac{2xy}{(x-y)^2} =0 \end{equation*}

and \(z(x,y)=\frac{x+y}{x-y}\) really does solve the PDE \(x\pdiff{z}{x}+ y\pdiff{z}{y}=0 \text{.}\)
