Let the four numbers be \(x_1\text{,}\) \(x_2\text{,}\) \(x_3\) and \(x_4\text{.}\) Let the four rounded numbers be \(x_1+\veps_1\text{,}\) \(x_2+\veps_2\text{,}\) \(x_3+\veps_3\) and \(x_4+\veps_4\text{.}\) If \(P(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)=x_1x_2x_3x_4\text{,}\) then the error in the product introduced by rounding is \(\big|P(x_1+\veps_1,x_2+\veps_2,x_3+\veps_3,x_4+\veps_4) -P(x_1,x_2,x_3,x_4)\big|\text{.}\)
