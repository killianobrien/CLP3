In this question we are going to find all solutions \(u(t,x)\) to the PDE

\begin{equation*} u_t=xu_x\qquad\text{for }x>0 \end{equation*}

that are of the special form \(u(x,t)= X(x)\,T(t)\text{,}\) with, for simplicity, \(X>0\) and \(T>0\text{.}\) We will use a technique called “separation of variables”.

  1. Show that \(u(x,t)= X(x)\,T(t)\text{,}\) with \(X\) and \(T\) nonzero, obeys the PDE \(u_t=xu_x\) if and only if
    \begin{equation*} \frac{T'(t)}{T(t)} = x\frac{X'(x)}{X(x)} \end{equation*}
  2. Show that \(\frac{T'(t)}{T(t)} = x\frac{X'(x)}{X(x)}\) if and only if there is a constant \(\la\) such that
    \begin{align*} T'(t)\amp=\la T(t) \\ X'(x)\amp=\frac{\la}{x} X(x) \end{align*}
  3. Find the general solutions to \(T'(t)=\la T(t)\) and \(X'(x)=\frac{\la}{x} X(x)\) with \(T,X>0\text{.}\)
