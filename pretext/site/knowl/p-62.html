For example, if \(\mathrm{i}(t)\) is the current in the electrical circuit shown in figure 1.7.1, then \(\mathrm{i}(t)\) satisfies the integral equation

\begin{equation*} L \frac{di}{dt} + Ri + \frac{1}{C} \int_0^1 i(t) \, dt = E_0 \sin(\omega t). \end{equation*}

If the circuit is switched on for a certain time period then the graph of the current might be shown by figure 1.7.2, or for a direct current (a battery say) powering the circuit, like that in figure 1.7.3.
